Investing.com – Despite a volatile week ending with a bounce on Friday, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq look set to close the week lower, following heavy losses in the tech sector.

Here are Investing.com’s stocks of the week: 

Walmart

Amid that volatility in tech stocks this week, investors sought safer assets, which helped push Walmart shares higher, up over 11% during the period. 

The rise has pushed the stock above $130, bringing Walmart’s valuation above $1 trillion. 

PayPal

PayPal shares tumbled more than 20% on Tuesday and are down over 23% for the week after the digital payments giant reported fourth quarter earnings and revenue that missed analyst expectations.

PYPL also announced a significant leadership change, with Enrique Lores appointed as President and CEO effective March 1, 2026, replacing Alex Chriss.

"While we had expected a potential miss and challenging 2026 guide, we note both came in worse than expected," wrote Wolfe Research analyst Darrin Peller in a note reacting to the report. He added that it has led to "greater questions around execution and market share/competition."

Novo Nordisk

Novo Nordisk’s stock sank over 14% on Tuesday, with further declines meaning it is down over 21% in the last week. 

The Wegovy-maker posted fourth-quarter results and issued a sales warning for 2026. 

“Following Trump MFN deals and new needed efforts to maintain access in the obesity market, Novo now faces extensive pricing headwinds in the US,” said BMO Capital analyst Evan Seigerman in a note. 

“While early signs of growth exist for the Wegovy pill, concessions for injectable GLP-1s are clearly impacting the topline offsetting gains in the oral market.”

Silicon Laboratories

SLAB surged over 48% on Wednesday and is set to end the week around 44% higher following news that the company will be acquired by Texas Instruments.

Texas Instruments will pay around $231 per share in an all-cash transaction, representing a total enterprise value of approximately $7.5 billion.

Strategy

After grinding lower for most of the week, MSTR bounced on Friday, currently up over 24%. However, the stock would still be set to end the week around 5% lower. 

The move tracked Bitcoin’s price, which plunged to a low of around $62,200 on Thursday, before recovering to above $70,100 today.

In a note to clients, Benchmark analyst Mark Palmer reiterated a Buy rating and $705 price target on the stock in a note on Friday that they remain bullish on the stock “based on a sum-of-the-parts analysis.

This “includes (1) the projected value of the company’s bitcoin holdings as of YE26, (2) a 10x multiple of its FY26E BTC $ Gain, and (3) the projected value of its software business as of YE26,” he wrote.  

“Our price target is based on our assumption that the price of bitcoin will reach $225,000 at YE26."

Amazon

Amazon shares plunged on Friday, currently down by more than 5% as investors reacted to the company’s latest quarterly report, released after Thursday’s close. 

The tech giant beat quarterly top-line estimates, but forecasted capital expenditures of about $200 billion for 2026, much higher than expected.

"AWS is accelerating with even faster growth ahead and Retail is delivering with improving efficiency. Yes, AMZN is investing (AWS, Retail, LEO), but it has a track record of showing ROIC, which leaves us bullish on this under-appreciated GenAI winner across," Morgan Stanley equity analyst Brian Nowak said in a note to clients.
